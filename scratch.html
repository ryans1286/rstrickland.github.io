Glacier and Ice Stream Bifurcation

I am investigating how topography, ice dynamics, and energy dissipation control the geometry and stability of glacier and ice stream bifurcations. This work combines observations, analytical theory, numerical modeling to understand why branching glacier systems in Antarctica and Greenland develop similar configurations across contrasting settings.

Ice Flow Over High-Relief Topography

I am using high-resolution subglacial topography maps and three-dimensional ice flow models to explore how bumps in the bed influences glacier motion, stress transmission, and the development of temperate ice. This research examines the extent to which rough topography influences ice flow on Thwaites Glacier, West Antarctica.

Antarctic Subglacial Groundwater

Examining the influence of deep groundwater systems beneath the Antarctic Ice Sheet and their potential role in basal lubrication, geothermal heat exchange, and long-term ice-stream dynamics. This work will couple a 3D ice flow model with subglacial drainage and deep groundwater flow models for Institute Ice Stream, West Antarctica.
