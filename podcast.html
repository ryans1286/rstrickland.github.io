About the Podcast

Ways of Ice explores glaciers as dynamic systems that shape landscapes, climate, and human understanding across deep time.

Combining glaciology, geomorphology, climate science, and the history of ideas, the series approaches Earth systems through narrative storytelling and scientific explanation.

Episodes range from Antarctic ice dynamics and mountain glaciers to the geometry of landscapes, the philosophy of modelling, and the history of glaciological thought.

🎙️ Season One: Glacial Thinking

The first season explores glaciers not only as physical systems, but as ways of understanding climate, landscape evolution, and planetary change.

Topics include erosion, ice ages, glacier geometry, scientific models, and the development of glaciology itself.
