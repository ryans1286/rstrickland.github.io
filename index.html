About Me

I am a quantiative glaciologist and geoscientist investigating feedbacks between glaciation, climate, and landscape evolution. Using 3D numerical glacier models and analytical methods, alongside targeted field observations, my work focuses on building process-based understandings of changes across Earth's ice sheets and mountain glaciers.

Currently, my research targets West Antarctica. My work contributes to two NERC-funded projects: the International Thwaites Glacier Collaboration (ITGC) TIME Project and the Impact of Deep Subglacial Groundwater on Ice Stream Flow in West Antarctica. Previously, I completed a PhD in Geoscience, specializing in modeling the evolution of debris-covered glaciers in High Mountain Asia. Before entering academia, I spent six years teaching mathematics and physics at an international school in California.

I am dedicated to open-science, ensuring that all modeling code, datasets, and physical parameters are publicly archived to support reproducible science.

Research featured in: National Geographic • The Geographer Magazine • Phys.org • The Copper River Record

Current Research

Glacier and Ice Stream Bifurcation

Investigating how interactions between topography, ice dynamics, and energy dissipation control the geomeetry and stability of glacier and ice stream bifurcations. This work combines analytical theory and numerical modeling to understand how branching glacier systems develop efficient flow geometries across complex landscapes.

Learn more →

Ice Flow Dynamics Over High-Relief Topography

Using high-resolution subglacial topography and three-dimensional ice flow models to explore how rugged topography influences glacier motion, stress transmission, and the development of temperate ice. This research examines the extent to which rough topography influences ice flow in Antarctica.

Learn more →

Antarctic Subglacial Groundwater

Examining the influence of deep groundwater systems beneath the Antarctic Ice Sheet and their potential role in basal lubrication, geothermal heat exchange, and long-term ice-stream dynamics. This work will couple 3D ice flow with subglacial drainage and groundwater flow.

🎙️ Ways of Ice Podcast

Ways of Ice explores glaciers as dynamic systems that shape landscapes, climate, and human understanding across deep time.

Written, produced, and hosted by Ryan M. Strickland. The series bridges technical glaciology and public-facing science through narrative storytelling, scientific modelling, and the history of ideas.

Episodes explore Antarctic ice dynamics, mountain glaciers, erosion, geometry, climate feedbacks, and the evolving ways humans have understood ice and planetary change.
