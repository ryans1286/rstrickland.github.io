About Me

I am a quantiative glaciologist and geoscientist investigating feedbacks between glaciation, climate, and landscape evolution. My research combines modelling, remote sensing, and fieldwork to build process-based understandings of Earth's ice sheets and mountain glaciers.

Currently, my research targets West Antarctica. My work contributes to two UK NERC-funded projects: the International Thwaites Glacier Collaboration (ITGC) TIME Project and the Impact of Deep Subglacial Groundwater on Ice Stream Flow in West Antarctica. Previously, I completed a PhD in Geoscience, specializing in modeling the evolution of debris-covered glaciers in High Mountain Asia. Before entering academia, I taught mathematics and physics at an international school in California for six years.

I am dedicated to open science, ensuring that all publications, modeling code, and datasets are publicly archived to support reproducible science.

Research featured in: National Geographic • The Geographer Magazine • Phys.org • The Copper River Record

🎙️ Ways of Ice Podcast

Ways of Ice explores glaciers as dynamic systems that shape landscapes, climate, and human understanding across deep time.

Written, produced, and hosted by Ryan M. Strickland. The series bridges technical glaciology and public-facing science through narrative storytelling, scientific modelling, and the history of ideas.

Episodes explore Antarctic ice dynamics, mountain glaciers, erosion, geometry, climate feedbacks, and the evolving ways humans have understood ice and planetary change.
